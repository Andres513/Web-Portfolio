Welcome to Tokyo

Also known as Tokyo Metropolis, this city is the capital of Japan and has a population of over 11 million people. An very interesting geographic about Tokyo is that the city is divided into 23 Special Wards, which are each governed as individual cities. Tokyo is also ranked first in the Global Economic Power Index and ranked first in the "Best Overall Experience" catergory of Trip Advisors World City Survey. The primary language spoken in Tokyo is Japanese. As of Tokyo's climate, its is rather similar to the US in terms of season. July through September are the hottest months with 80 degress Fahrenheit being the daily high temperatures. It is often stated that it is very humid around this time of year as well. People often say that you can walk and swim at the same time. The coldest months are January and February. There are many famous attractions in Tokyo, one of them being the Tokyo SkyTree. Another attraction is the Tokyo National Museum. It was established in 1872 and is one of the largwst art musuems in the world. The museum collects, houses and preserves a collection of art works and archaeological findings of Asia, focusing on Japan. As for the history of Tokyo, it first started out as a small fishing village arount 3000 B.C. and was first known as Edo. It was fortified in the 12th century and became home to Edo Castle, which is now known as the Imperial Palace (One of the many attractions listed to your left). This city has a high attraction for immigration and is an amazing place to visit. There is a lot of diversity and culture that not only Shinto offers, but the cultural blend that the tourists provides was well. This is a place you should travel to.
